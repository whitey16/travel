Hong Kong

"Same Same, But Different."

4.27.17 - 4.30.17

We arrived in Hong Kong at about 9:30 in the morning. The Hong Kong airport is to the northwest of the city, but it is very easy to get into town via the Airport Express. Just head to the ticket counter and grab an express ticket for about 5 bucks per person or so. We stayed in a hotel for this spot. A pretty nice one at that, called the Harbour Grand Kowloon. We got a pretty decent deal though, at about $150/night, which is not too shabby given the hotel and the location. There are definitely cheaper options, but we wanted to start the trip with a bit of luxury. The hotel is located on the Kowloon side of the harbour, and offers some spectacular views of the Hong Kong skyline. Rooftop pool was pretty sick, even though the “whirlpool” was just a pool with bubbles, not a hot tub...lame.

About the Title

Throughout Asia we saw a lot of people wearing shirts that read "Same, same, but different." We weren't quite sure whether this was a common phrase, or what, but we felt that phrase perfectly encapsulated Hong Kong because we felt like it would be an extremely diffent feeling city, but it turned out to be so much like many large cities back home, that it truly felt like it was just the same, but in a different location.
